01  /  The shape of the work

Infrastructure work. Reporting work. Compliance work. Deal-structuring work. All of it growing while the team stays the same size.

Space Florida runs a 220-project, $6 billion aerospace pipeline, a record launch cadence at the nation's busiest spaceport, and a workforce program in 185 high schools. That is infrastructure work. It is also reporting work, compliance work, and deal-structuring work that grows every year while the team stays roughly the same size.

The 2022 operational audit named the seams. Cooperative agreements, public-deposit workflows, and committee meeting discipline. Those findings were addressed. The underlying shape of the work did not change.

02  /  Three layers

Sixty. Thirty. Ten. Most agencies get the ratio wrong, then wonder why the model hallucinates the bond covenant.

60%

Ordinary database and workflow automation.

Deal tracking, status rollups, cooperative agreement versioning, board-pack generation, legislative reporting. Postgres-and-Python territory, done right. This is where the week actually goes.

30%

Rule-based logic.

State procurement thresholds. Public deposit rules. Bond covenants. FOIA response timelines. These rules do not want a language model guessing at them. They want a rules engine with a clear audit trail.

10%

Where language models genuinely help.

Reading long PDF agreements against a checklist. Summarizing committee transcripts into board minutes. Triaging inbound vendor inquiries against a pipeline. Drafting legislative narrative from structured data. That ten percent compounds when the other ninety are solid underneath it.

04  /  The methodology is published

Every decision leaves a trail an auditor can read top to bottom.

Interpretable Context Methodology treats agent context as a layered filesystem. L0 identity through L4 working artifacts. Submitted to ACM Transactions on Interactive Intelligent Systems. When a state auditor pulls a thread on how a deal got structured, the entire decision context is inspectable. That is the right shape for a state special district subject to Chapter 331 and public-records law.

A second instrument, the Ethics Engine, is a psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. For agencies fielding AI governance questions from boards and legislatures, it gives a defensible answer where most vendors offer a slide.
